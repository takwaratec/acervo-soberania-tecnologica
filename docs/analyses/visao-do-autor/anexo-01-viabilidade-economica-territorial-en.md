## Abstract

This instrument operationalizes the economic feasibility and territorial continuity framework developed across the series of Notebooks on Ecological Revision and Technological Sovereignty (Notebooks 1 to 7). The model structures data to compare economic and operational scenarios without presupposing the financial viability of a manufacturing unit, cooperative, pyrolysis reactor, structural component, or biomass co-product. The framework comprises pre-modeling prerequisites, three mandatory scenarios (restrictive, baseline, and conditioned expansion), detailed itemization of CAPEX (initial capital investment), OPEX (operating expenses), cash cycle and working capital, demand and revenue evidence, cross-subsidy rules, public funding/credit sources, 12 calculated indicators, and 7 formal decision gates. The tool prevents artificial viability claims based on unpaid community labor or arbitrary co-product pricing, affirming that resizing, sharing infrastructure, or terminating a project before incurring community debt constitutes a legitimate technical outcome.

**Keywords:** economic feasibility; territorial continuity; CAPEX; OPEX; working capital; scenario analysis; social technology; technological sovereignty.

---

## 1. Purpose

This instrument operationalizes a key question from Notebooks 3, 5, and 6:

> **Can the proposed technical capacity remain active without transferring debt, risk, or invisible dependence onto the territory?**

The model structures information required to compare scenarios. It does not assume that a workshop, factory, cooperative, reactor, component, or co-product is viable. It recognizes as legitimate outcomes:

- resizing production capacity;
- sharing infrastructure;
- postponing capital acquisition;
- testing a single module;
- selecting alternative technology;
- terminating the project before generating debt.

The complementary spreadsheet (`modelo-viabilidade-economica-territorial-v0.1.xlsx`) separates editable inputs, calculations, cash flow, sensitivity, decision criteria, and sources. Empty cells represent data to be collected, not permission to insert arbitrary estimates.
