## Abstract

This technical manual presents the design principles, structural behavior, and execution protocols of "Plant-Based Weld" joints (*Solda Vegetal*). Developed specifically for round bamboo culms, this methodology combines high-density Castor Oil Polyurethane (PU) biocomposites with fiber-reinforced collar wraps. It eliminates the severe stress concentrations and longitudinal splitting associated with conventional steel bolt connections, enabling modular prefabrication for sustainable social housing (ATHIS) and industrial infrastructure.

---

## 1. Introduction and Structural Mechanics of Bamboo Connections

Round bamboo culms are naturally optimized structural elements featuring anisotropic geometry, hollow cross-sections, and high tensile strength parallel to the fibers. However, their weakness in transverse shear and cleavage parallel to the grain makes traditional mechanical fasteners (such as through-bolts) prone to premature longitudinal splitting under shear or bending loads.

The "Plant-Based Weld" system replaces point-load bolt fasteners with a continuous, expansive polymer matrix joint. By anchoring into the inner culm wall and enveloping the exterior node with resin-impregnated fiber wraps, joint forces are distributed uniformly across the entire structural node.

---

## 2. Mechanical Performance and The "Plant-Based Weld" System

### 2.1 Interface Dynamics and Internal Core Filling
- **Compressive Buckling Prevention:** High-density Castor Oil PU foam ($> 200 	ext{ kg/m}^3$) is injected into the node chamber, preventing localized inward wall buckling under flexural loads.
- **Interfacial Shear Strength:** Steam-alkalinized inner culm walls ($0 	ext{ Barg}$ saturate lime vapor) expose cellulose microfibrils, creating high mechanical interlocking and chemical bonding with the PU matrix.
- **Flexural Efficiency:** Jointed assemblies retain over $85\%$ of the continuous culm bending moment capacity, exceeding requirements of ABNT NBR 16828-1 and ISO 22157.

---

## 3. The "Plant-Based Weld" Technology and PU Resin Matrix

### 3.1 Thermal and Chemical Conditioning Interdependence
Saturated steam conditioning ($0 	ext{ Barg}$) removes non-structural waxes and starches, raising the surface free energy necessary for hydrophobic polyurethane adhesion.

### 3.2 Biological Protection with Pyroligneous Acid
Concentrated pyroligneous extract applied during thermal treatment delivers long-term resistance against wood-boring insects (*Dinoderus minutus*) without toxic synthetic chemicals.

---

## 4. Modular Prefabrication: Structural Panels and Spatial Systems

Standardized "Plant-Based Weld" nodes allow rapid field assembly of prefabricated wall panels, space frames, and roof trusses. This modular approach ("Bio-sovereignty Lego") drastically reduces construction time and eliminates specialized heavy machinery requirements.
