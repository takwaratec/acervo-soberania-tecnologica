## Abstract

This annex presents the structured financial and economic modeling matrix designed to evaluate territorial bioeconomy biorefineries, rural bamboo processing units (UBPs), and socio-environmental housing initiatives. It integrates CAPEX, OPEX, cash flow cycles, cross-subsidization without hidden liabilities, and economic passage criteria across TRL-1 to TRL-7.

---

## 1. Purpose

This instrument establishes a standardized methodology to evaluate the economic sustainability, cash flow stability, and territorial scalability of decentralized bamboo processing facilities and biochar/polyurethane applications. It prevents artificial profitability claims, accounts for local biomass logistics, and ensures transparent public funding accountability.

---

## 2. Baseline Assumptions Prior to Modeling

1. **Local Biomass Sourcing Radius:** Maximum transport distance of 30 km to minimize logistics emissions and freight costs.
2. **Autothermal Energy Balance:** Pyrolysis off-gases fuel the thermal reactor (T01/T02) and wood vinegar extraction without external fossil fuel inputs during steady-state operation.
3. **Traceability Compliance:** All harvested culms must adhere to NBR 16828-1/2 forest management criteria.

---

## 3. Mandatory Scenarios

- **Scenario A (Baseline / Conservative):** Operates at 50% installed capacity, using standard commercial market rates for polyurethane reagents and local transport costs.
- **Scenario B (Optimized Operational):** Operates at 85% capacity with integrated co-product monetization (wood vinegar, biochar soil amendment, structural culms).
- **Scenario C (Public Policy / Housing Grant Integration):** Incorporates public social housing subsidies (ATHIS/MCMV) for structural component supply.

---

---

## 5. Operational Expenditure (OPEX)

1. **Biomass Procurement:** Raw bamboo culms purchased from managed local stands.
2. **Chemical Reagents:** Vegetable Polyurethane (castor oil polyol + MDI polyisocyanate) and wood ash supply.
3. **Labor & Operator Stipends:** Technical operators, field harvesters, and quality control staff.
4. **Maintenance & Utility Reserves:** Periodic maintenance for valves, seals, pumps, and electrical power.

---

## 6. Working Capital and Cash Flow Cycle

- **Inventory Holding Period:** 15 days for raw culm conditioning, 5 days for curing.
- **Receivables Cycle:** 30 days standard payment terms for public/private off-takers.
- **Minimum Cash Reserve:** 60 days of operational OPEX maintained to buffer against seasonal harvest delays.

---

## 7. Demand, Revenue Streams, and Co-products

1. **Structural Bamboo Components:** Insulated structural culms injected with bio-PU.
2. **Pyroligneous Acid (Wood Vinegar):** Agricultural biopesticide and organic soil conditioner.
3. **Biochar:** Soil amendment for carbon sequestration and eco-brick aggregate.
4. **Bio-Bricks:** Soil-cement-biochar structural masonry units for local construction.

---

## 8. Cross-Subsidization Without Hidden Liabilities

Cross-subsidization between commercial sales (high-value structural poles and biopesticide) and social housing supply (ATHIS components) must be explicitly recorded in accounting logs without concealing logistics or waste disposal liabilities.

---

## 9. Public Funding, Credit Lines, and Continuity

Projects transitioning through TRL-4 to TRL-7 may leverage FINEP Mais Inovacao, FAPESP PIPE, or regional bioeconomy credit lines. All capital grants must be segregated from operational revenues to verify self-sustaining OPEX capability.

---

## 10. Key Financial Metrics

- **Net Present Value (NPV):** Calculated at a 10% annual discount rate over a 5-year horizon.
- **Internal Rate of Return (IRR):** Targeted at > 18% for Scenario B.
- **Payback Period:** Target of 2.5 to 3.5 years for initial CAPEX recovery.
- **Break-Even Volume:** Minimum monthly production threshold required to cover fixed OPEX.

---

## 11. Economic Gate Criteria for TRL Passage

- **Gate TRL-3 -> TRL-4:** Unit cost per linear meter of treated/injected culm lower than conventional steel/concrete structural equivalents.
- **Gate TRL-4 -> TRL-5:** Positive operational cash flow achieved over 3 consecutive pilot batches.
- **Gate TRL-5 -> TRL-6:** Formal supply contracts executed with certified off-takers covering 60% of annual plant capacity.

---

## 12. Decision Audit Log Template

Every pilot deployment must log:
- Date of evaluation.
- Assigned scenario (A, B, or C).
- Approved CAPEX/OPEX variations.
- Responsible technical manager signature.

---

## 13. Integration Across Notebooks 1 to 7

- **Notebook 1 (Preservation):** Input costs for eco-friendly treatment.
- **Notebook 2 (ICT Technical Specs):** Testing standards impacting QC rejection rates and material waste.
- **Notebook 3 (Territorial Production):** Multi-unit replication and distributed logistics.
- **Notebook 4 (Compliance & Standards):** Certification costs (IPT/NBR).
- **Notebook 5 (ATHIS & Post-Occupancy):** Social housing cost targets.
- **Notebook 6 (Cascade Biochar):** Revenue models for co-products.
- **Notebook 7 (Crises & Closing Loops):** Life-cycle assessment financial impacts.
